# Abhilash — Deep Knowledge File
> Business Head / GM | EV Battery Swapping & Energy-as-a-Service
> Last updated: April 2026

---

## Profile

Abhilash is the Business Head for an EV battery swapping / energy-as-a-service business. He is the **final decision maker** across all functions — operations, franchise, finance, tech, HR, and fraud. He directly coordinates with 5–15 people spanning field ops, franchise partners, finance, tech, customer support, and HR. His day starts by self-pulling data from multiple dashboards, reports, emails, and WhatsApp. Information is fragmented across sources with no single source of truth. He context-switches constantly across 14 domains, prioritizing by urgency and business impact.

**Communication style:** Data-first — numbers before opinions.
**Core frustration:** Too much time chasing updates from people; no unified view of the business.

## Domain 1: Active Users, Onboarding & Swaps

### Where He Looks
- A **daily report sent by someone** on the team

### What He Sees
- Active user count
- Yesterday's onboarding numbers
- Yesterday's total swap count

### What He Compares Against
- **Monthly target** — is the business on pace?

### What He's Looking For
- Onboarding number dropped vs previous day → immediate flag

### Steps After Spotting a Drop
1. Identify **which city/region** the drop happened in
2. Identify **which franchise or station** it's linked to
3. Determine if it's a **field issue or app/tech issue**
4. Identify **who the responsible owner** is
5. Send a **WhatsApp message to the responsible person** directly

### What He Expects as Resolution
- Root cause explanation from the owner
- Check if it's a **one-day blip or a developing trend**
- **Recovery target set for the next day**
- Corrective action plan from the owner

### Takeaway Signal
> Onboarding drop = city + station + owner + cause + next-day recovery plan. Not closed until trend reverses.

## Domain 2: Station Openings, Plans & Execution

### Where He Looks
- **Combination of a sheet (pipeline tracker) + WhatsApp** updates from franchise/ops team

### What He Sees
- Station pipeline across two lifecycle models:
  - Model A: Site identification → Agreement signing → Setup → Testing → Go-live
  - Model B: Lead → Negotiation → Onboarding → Soft launch → Full ops

### What He's Looking For
- Stations **stuck at a stage too long**
- **Specific bottleneck stages**: agreement/legal delays, hardware procurement delays, site readiness/civil work delays
- **Expected go-live vs actual go-live gap**

### Steps After Spotting a Stuck Station
1. Send a **WhatsApp message to the responsible person** asking for an update
2. Escalate via **direct call or to franchise partner** if severity warrants
3. Get a **new expected date**
4. Track it until unblocked
5. **Escalate to leadership** if the gap vs expected go-live is too large

### Most Common Bottlenecks
- Hardware procurement / delivery delays
- Site readiness / civil work delays
- Agreement / legal delays

### Takeaway Signal
> Every stuck station needs: reason for delay + new ETA + owner accountability. Escalates to leadership when go-live gap becomes business-critical.

## Domain 3: Stockouts

### Where He Looks
- A **daily stockout report** sent to him

### What He Sees Per Stockout
- Which station and city
- How many batteries are missing / short
- How long the stockout has been ongoing
- Root cause: supply delay, theft, damage, or high demand
- Revenue lost due to the stockout

### Immediate Action
- **Flags it in the ops group chat** for the ops team to resolve

### What He Expects as Resolution
- **ETA for restock within hours** — not days
- Ops team to coordinate emergency restock or redistribution

### Closure Criteria
- ETA received and committed to
- Stockout no longer appears in next day's report

### Most Common Root Causes
- Supply chain / procurement not ordering in time
- Batteries damaged or not returned by users

### Prevention Status
- **No formal prevention system** — currently reactive
- No minimum inventory thresholds or demand-based pre-positioning yet

### Takeaway Signal
> Stockout = station + city + cause + revenue impact + restock ETA. Reactive today; prevention system is a known gap.

## Domain 4: Free Swaps, kWh, Revenue, Topline, Bottomline

### Where He Looks
- A **revenue / finance dashboard**

### What He Sees
- Count of free swaps
- kWh per swap per user
- Revenue source split
- **Topline vs monthly target**
- **Bottomline / margin after costs**

### What He's Looking For
- Topline trend **day-over-day** — is revenue growing or declining?
- **Revenue leakage or billing gaps**
- **kWh consumption vs swap count mismatch** — signals energy misuse or billing error

### Steps After Spotting an Anomaly
1. **Check with ops** if swap data matches billing data
2. If mismatch confirmed → ask ops for explanation
3. Expect **same-day explanation** from the responsible owner
4. **Escalate to leadership** if bottomline impact is significant
5. **Track until month closes** and reconcile fully

### On High Free Swaps
- Primary suspicion: **field staff operationally granting free swaps** without authorization
- Investigates who issued them, at which station, and why

### Takeaway Signal
> Revenue anomaly = ops data vs billing cross-check first. Same-day explanation expected. Escalates if margin is impacted. Month not closed until reconciled.

## Domain 5: Cash in Hand

### Where He Looks
- A **cash collection report sent by finance**

### What He Sees Per Person
- Who is holding cash and how much
- How many days the cash has been sitting
- Reason given for the delay

### Who Typically Holds Cash
- Field staff collecting but not depositing
- Franchise partners holding collected fees
- Station managers sitting on daily collections
- Multiple people in the chain each holding partial amounts

### Intervention Threshold
- **Depends on amount** — higher amount = lower tolerance for delay
- No fixed day threshold; amount-driven escalation

### How He Intervenes
- **Messages the person's manager / supervisor** (not the person directly)

### Closure Criteria
- Person gives a reason and **commits to a transfer date**
- **Finance confirms receipt** on their end

### Takeaway Signal
> Cash sitting = amount × days = risk. He goes through the supervisor. Closed only when finance confirms receipt — not when person promises.

## Domain 6: Franchise Groups & Issues

### Where He Looks
- **All of**: franchise pipeline tracker (sheet/dashboard) + WhatsApp groups per franchise + direct calls and messages with franchise partners

### Lifecycle Stages He's Personally Involved In
- Closing new franchise deals
- Agreement signing and onboarding
- Station setup and go-live
- Daily station operations and performance
- Franchise partner disputes or escalations
- Franchise payment and financial compliance

### Most Common Issues
- Station underperforming vs committed targets
- Financial disputes or payment delays from franchise
- Franchise trying to exit or renegotiate terms
- Franchise partner going silent / unresponsive
- Franchise not following operational SOPs

### Escalation Sequence for Silent / Underperforming Partner
1. **Sends a team member to meet them in person** (first line of escalation — not a message)

### For Financial Disputes / Exit Attempts
1. **Negotiates directly** with the partner
2. **Involves legal/finance** for formal resolution
3. **Documents everything** for accountability

### Accountability Mechanism
- **Penalty clauses for SOP violations** exist in franchise agreements

### Takeaway Signal
> Franchise issues get a physical visit first. Financial disputes go legal + documented. Penalty clauses are the formal lever. Abhilash is hands-on across the entire franchise lifecycle.

## Domain 7: Customer Support Tickets

### Where He Looks
- **WhatsApp escalations from the CS team** (not a dashboard — reactive and people-driven)

### What He Reviews
- Volume of open tickets
- Ticket type / category breakdown
- Which city or station tickets are from
- How long tickets have been open (TAT)
- Repeat complaints on the same issue

### Most Common Ticket Types
- Battery quality complaints
- Station unavailability / stockout-related complaints
- Billing or payment disputes

### What Triggers His Personal Involvement
- Repeat complaints on the **same issue from the same location**
- Tickets **open beyond acceptable TAT**
- A complaint that **could become a public / social media issue**

### What He Does When Involved
1. **Routes it to the relevant team** (ops / tech / finance) to fix the root cause
2. **Identifies the systemic fix** and assigns it as a task to an owner

### How He Tracks CS Health
- **Category-wise ticket volume trend** — is a category growing?
- **Whether the same issue recurs** after resolution — is the fix actually working?

### Takeaway Signal
> CS is healthy when volume is flat/falling and fixes hold. He intervenes on repeat patterns, TAT breaches, and reputational risk. Resolution = root cause fixed, not just ticket closed.

## Domain 10: Fraud Detection

### How Fraud Is Discovered
- **Combination of all**: anomalies he spots in data himself + fraud alerts/reports sent to him + field team or franchise flagging suspicious activity

### Most Common Fraud Patterns
- Free swaps issued fraudulently by field staff
- Cash collected but not deposited
- Swap count manipulation at station level
- Fake user onboarding to hit targets
- Franchise misreporting revenue or usage

### Investigation Process
1. **Assigns someone** to investigate and report back (does not investigate alone)
2. **Involves HR/legal immediately** — does not wait to confirm before looping them in

### Outcomes Once Fraud Is Confirmed
Depends on severity:
- Warning and monitoring period
- Process change to close the loophole
- Financial recovery / clawback
- Termination / exit of the person or franchise

### Proactive Prevention Mechanisms
- **Pattern recognition over time** in reports — he looks for recurring anomalies
- **Whistleblower reports** from team members

### Post-Closure Action
- **Reports to leadership** with full impact assessment

### Takeaway Signal
> Fraud = investigate via assigned person + HR/legal looped in immediately. Outcome severity-matched. Post-closure = leadership report with impact. Prevention is pattern-based, not system-automated yet.

## Domain 11: Procurement

### Where He Looks
- A **procurement tracker / sheet**

### What He Tracks
- Batteries / hardware for stations
- Spare parts for maintenance
- Station setup materials (civil, electrical)
- Vendor onboarding for new suppliers

### Most Common Delays
- Order not placed on time
- Payment hold causing vendor to pause supply
- Wrong item or quantity delivered
- Quality issues on delivery

### How He Unblocks Delays
Depends on delay type:
- **Messages procurement team** to expedite
- **Contacts vendor directly**
- **Escalates payment to finance** to unblock

### How He Connects Procurement to Operations
- **Links procurement to station go-live dates** — hardware must arrive before go-live
- **Reviews open POs vs delivery ETAs** — checks if delivery timelines are at risk

### Closure Criteria
- Vendor delivers on revised ETA
- Finance confirms payment released
- Item quality verified on receipt

### Takeaway Signal
> Procurement is tracked proactively against go-live dates. Delays get unblocked through the right lever — procurement, vendor, or finance depending on root cause.

## Domain 12: Finance

### Where He Looks
- **Emails and WhatsApp** — finance updates come to him via these channels, not a dashboard

### What He Reviews Regularly
- Invoices raised vs collected
- Vendor payments due or overdue
- Monthly P&L vs budget
- Bank reconciliation gaps

### Most Common Finance Issues
- Vendor payments delayed causing supply chain impact
- Franchise fees not collected on time
- Unexplained entries or mismatches in reconciliation

### How He Handles Franchise Fee Issues
- **Payment due TO franchise**: asks finance to release in the next immediate payment cycle
- **Payment due FROM franchise**: delegates to field ops to collect the payment soon

### How He Handles Vendor Payment Delays
1. **Asks finance to release payment immediately**
2. **Escalates to leadership** if vendor threatens to stop supply

### How He Handles Reconciliation Mismatches
- **Asks finance for a reconciliation explanation** — does not trace it himself

### Takeaway Signal
> Finance flows through email and WhatsApp — no dashboard. He routes franchise fee issues through finance or field ops depending on direction of payment. Recon mismatches go back to finance to explain.

## Domain 14: P&L Ownership

### Review Frequency
- **Monthly** formal review (tracks daily revenue metrics but formal P&L is monthly)

### Key Line Items He Watches
- Revenue vs target
- Cost of energy (kWh)
- Franchise fee collections
- Manpower costs
- Procurement / hardware costs
- Customer acquisition cost

### When P&L Tracks Below Target — His Actions
All of the following, simultaneously:
1. **Cuts costs** in specific underperforming line items
2. **Pushes revenue levers** — more swaps, onboarding push, pricing review
3. **Escalates to leadership** with a recovery plan
4. **Holds team leads accountable** for their respective cost/revenue lines

### Takeaway Signal
> P&L below target triggers a full-stack response: cost cuts + revenue push + leadership escalation + team accountability. He owns the number end-to-end.

## Operating Principles

| Principle | Evidence |
|-----------|----------|
| **Self-serve data** | Pulls dashboards himself every morning rather than waiting |
| **Numbers before narrative** | Data-first communication style to leadership |
| **Route to the right lever** | Procurement delay → finance/vendor/ops depending on cause |
| **Supervisor-level accountability** | Cash issues go to the manager, not the person holding cash |
| **Physical escalation for franchise** | Sends a person to meet, not just a message |
| **Legal-first on fraud** | HR/legal looped in immediately, before confirmation |
| **Month doesn't close until reconciled** | Revenue anomalies tracked through to month-end |
| **Prevention is a known gap** | Stockout and fraud prevention are reactive — he knows this |
